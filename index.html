01 — Research

A building generates data. An ontology turns it into knowledge. An agent turns knowledge into action.

Buildings are among the most complex systems society operates — long-lived, unique, and run by many hands. My work gives their digital twins a semantic backbone: formal, machine-readable descriptions of every sensor, model, and service, so that AI agents can interpret, monitor, and manage building systems across their entire lifecycle.

Semantic layer

Ontology & metadata engineering

Extending the Brick schema with new classes, relationships, and properties — from virtual sensors to dynamic, weighted ontologies whose entities respond to faults, model error, and system change over time.

Sensing layer

Virtual sensors for HVAC systems

Model-based observation, backup, and validation sensors that estimate what physical hardware cannot measure — keeping district heating and chilled-water systems observable when devices drift or fail.

Service layer

AI-agent-driven building services

LLM- and ontology-driven agents that generate system ontologies, retrieve metadata, detect and diagnose faults, and recommend operational actions — toward truly agentic built environments.

04 — About

From circuits in Dhaka to digital twins in Suwon.

I trained as an electrical and electronic engineer at East West University in Bangladesh, harvesting energy from footsteps for my undergraduate thesis. Today, at the Building Information Science and Technology (BIST) Lab under Prof. Sungmin Yoon, I work at the intersection of smart cities, HVAC systems, and knowledge engineering.

My master's thesis explored the user-focused servitization of digital twins through metadata schemas, ontological frameworks, and adaptive visualizations — a thread I now carry into my PhD, asking how buildings and cities can describe, monitor, and eventually manage themselves.
